Field Report · Flag Pond, Tennessee

We don't ship it. We cut it and drive it to you.

Most "fresh" mushrooms on a kitchen's back dock are already three, four, five days off the block — trucked through a distributor, sweating in a case. Ours are cut the same morning they reach your door. That's the whole idea, and it's why it tastes different. John walks the grow room at first light and cuts only what's ordered and at its peak that day, packs it cold into clean, breathable packaging, and drives it out on a set weekly route you can count on.

Hours, not days. The Flag Pond delivery schedule

Fresher mushrooms last longer on your shelf, hold better on the plate, and cut your spoilage. Freshness isn't a slogan here — it's the delivery schedule, printed and kept.

    Editor's Letter

    A new farm, an old promise: fresh and reliable.

    Flag Pond Mushroom Farm is a small growing operation tucked in the mountains of Unicoi County, Tennessee, just north of the North Carolina line — built and run by John Oliver, one clean flush at a time.

    We're new, and we're not going to pretend otherwise. What we offer isn't a decade of awards; it's something a lot of kitchens have trouble finding: mushrooms that are genuinely fresh, a grower who actually calls you back, and a delivery day you can set your prep list by. When you order from us, you're talking to the person who grew it.

    Everything we grow is cut here, by hand, the morning it goes to you. That's a promise we can actually keep — and it's the whole reason the farm exists.

    — John, Flag Pond Mushroom Farm
