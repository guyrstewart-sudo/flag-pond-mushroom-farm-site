What We Grow

Six gourmet mushrooms, grown to order.

We grow a tight, chef-driven lineup so every flush is at its peak. Tell us what your menu needs and we'll grow into it — this is a pull system, not a warehouse.

Lion's Mane Flagship

Big, snow-white pom-pom heads with a sweet, seafood-like flavor — pan-seared like a scallop or pulled like crab. Our signature crop and the one chefs ask for by name.

Great for: crab-cake swaps, steak-style medallions, vegan "lobster" rolls.

Blue & Grey Oyster

Tender, fast-cooking clusters with a mild, savory bite. The dependable everyday oyster — beautiful sautéed, roasted, or crisped on a flat-top.

Great for: sautés, tacos, grain bowls, "fried oyster mushroom" apps.

Golden & Pink Oyster

Sunburst yellow and coral pink, with a slightly deeper, almost bacon-y note when seared hard. The color that makes a plate — or a market display — pop.

Great for: specials, farmers-market color, seasonal plating.

Shiitake

Meaty caps with a rich, umami depth that stands up to any protein — the classic gourmet mushroom, grown fresh instead of shipped in dried.

Great for: broths, braises, stir-fry, mushroom "bacon".

Chestnut

Firm little caps on slender stems with a nutty crunch that holds its texture when cooked. A kitchen workhorse that keeps its shape and bite.

Great for: roasting whole, pasta, pizza, hearty sides.

Dried Lion's Mane

Our flagship, dehydrated at the farm for a long shelf life and concentrated flavor. Perfect for retail bags, off-season menus, teas and pantry stock.

Great for: grocery shelf, powders, rehydrated for soups & sauces.

The Fresh-Morning Harvest

We don't ship it. We cut it and drive it to you.

Most "fresh" mushrooms on a kitchen's back dock are already three, four, five days off the block — trucked through a distributor, sweating in a case. Ours are cut the same morning they reach your door. That's the whole idea, and it's why it tastes different.

  • Before dawn
    We harvest

    John walks the grow room at first light and cuts only what's ordered and at its peak that day.

  • Morning
    We pack cold

    Straight into clean, breathable packaging — no long cold-chain, no middle-man warehouse.

  • Same day
    We deliver

    Into your kitchen or cooler within hours, on a set weekly route you can count on.

Hours,
not days.

Fresher mushrooms last longer on your shelf, hold better on the plate, and cut your spoilage. Freshness isn't a slogan here — it's the delivery schedule.

About the Farm

A new farm, an old promise: fresh and reliable.

Flag Pond Mushroom Farm is a small growing operation tucked in the mountains of Unicoi County, Tennessee, just north of the North Carolina line — built and run by John Oliver, one clean flush at a time.

We're new, and we're not going to pretend otherwise. What we offer isn't a decade of awards; it's something a lot of kitchens have trouble finding: mushrooms that are genuinely fresh, a grower who actually calls you back, and a delivery day you can set your prep list by. When you order from us, you're talking to the person who grew it.

Everything we grow is cut here, by hand, the morning it goes to you. That's a promise we can actually keep — and it's the whole reason the farm exists.

— John, Flag Pond Mushroom Farm
